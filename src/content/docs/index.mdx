## The core invariant

An artifact reaches consumers only if it is byte-identical to what was validated, carries re-verifiable attestations (SLSA provenance, cosign signature, SBOM, vulnerability report), and publication is fail-closed gated on verification.

## What you will find here

The documentation is organized by [Diátaxis](https://diataxis.fr/) — learning, tasks, reference, and understanding kept separate.

- **[Ecosystem Hub](/docs/ecosystem/)** — the map of the whole ecosystem: every repo, template, and tool, with links out to each one's own docs.
- **[Tutorial](/docs/tutorials/verify-your-first-attested-release/)** — verify your first attested release, hands-on, from a clean workstation.
- **[How-to Guides](/docs/guides/onboard-a-repo/)** — onboard a repo to the quality gates, verify a release independently, and promote a build by digest.
- **[Reference](/docs/reference/quality-gate-workflows/)** — the central reusable workflows, the signing/verification seam, and the catalog-updater, contract by contract.
- **[Concepts](/docs/concepts/01-the-digest-is-the-release/)** — focused articles on the design decisions behind attested delivery: digest identity, attestation survival across registry hops, admission enforcement, SLSA levels, SBOMs, supply-chain hazards, DORA, AI provenance, and pipeline observability.
- **[Specifications](/docs/specifications/research-report/)** — formal specs for the promotion pipeline, interface contracts, production-readiness gates, and GitHub-native quality gates.
- **[Architecture Decisions](/docs/adrs/0001-attestation-preserving-digest-promotion/)** — the ADRs recording the rationale behind every significant technical choice, from digest promotion to security tooling pins.
